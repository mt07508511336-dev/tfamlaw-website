Scottish Legal Aid Fees Set to Increase by 13% This September

21 July 2026

Access to justice is a cornerstone of a fair society, ensuring that everyone, regardless of their financial situation, can seek legal assistance when needed. In Scotland, legal aid plays a pivotal role in this, providing funding for those who might otherwise be unable to afford legal representation.

Recently, the Scottish Government announced a significant development: a 13% increase in legal aid fees, set to take effect from September 2026. This uplift is part of a broader initiative to support solicitors who offer legal aid services and to enhance the overall legal aid system. The increase is designed to make legal aid work more sustainable for solicitors, encouraging more legal professionals to participate in the scheme and thereby improving access to justice for the public.

In addition to the fee increase, the government has introduced funding for up to 40 legal aid traineeships. This move aims to attract new talent into the legal aid sector, ensuring its vitality and sustainability for the future. Furthermore, there is a commitment to providing digital support to help law firms invest in technology that can assist trainees in their work and court appearances.

These measures come in response to ongoing concerns about the availability of legal aid services across Scotland. Reports have highlighted a decline in the number of solicitor firms offering legal aid, leading to so-called "legal aid deserts" where individuals struggle to find legal representation. By increasing fees and supporting new trainees, the government hopes to reverse this trend and ensure that legal aid services are accessible to all who need them.

At Thompson Family Law, we welcome these developments. As a family-run firm with offices in Glasgow (Bishopbriggs), Edinburgh, Coatbridge, and Airdrie, we understand the importance of accessible legal services. We believe that these changes will not only support solicitors but also, more importantly, benefit clients by ensuring they have access to the legal assistance they require.

It's essential for clients to be aware of these changes and understand how they might impact the availability and quality of legal aid services.
