Recent Legal Aid Reforms in Scotland: What You Need to Know

24 July 2026

At Thompson Family Law, we understand that navigating the legal system can be challenging, especially when financial constraints are involved. That's why we're committed to keeping you informed about recent developments in legal aid that may impact you.

In January 2026, the Scottish Government announced a significant 13% increase in legal aid fees and fixed payments, set to take effect in September 2026. This adjustment aims to better support solicitors who provide essential legal services to those in need. Additionally, funding has been allocated for up to 40 Legal Aid Traineeship positions, along with digital support to help law firms equip trainees with the necessary tools for their work and court appearances. These measures are part of a broader effort to modernize and sustain the legal aid system in Scotland. Source

Since 2019, cumulative fee increases have surpassed 25%, outpacing inflation. The most recent rise in April 2023 brought total additional funding to £31 million since 2019. These investments reflect a commitment to ensuring that legal aid remains accessible and that legal professionals are adequately supported in their roles.

For individuals seeking legal assistance, these reforms mean that more solicitors may be available to take on legal aid cases, potentially reducing wait times and improving access to justice. If you're facing a legal issue and are concerned about costs, it's worth exploring whether you qualify for legal aid under these updated provisions.
