How the "daily blog" piece actually works

A plain website like this one is static — the files just sit on a server and don't do anything by themselves. To get a genuinely new post every day, something needs to run on a schedule: gather a topic, write about it, and add it to this page automatically.

That's a small piece of automation sitting alongside the website, not part of the website itself. It's very doable, but it needs a decision on where it runs (for example, a free scheduled task on GitHub, or a paid automation service), and it should write original commentary rather than copy other publishers' articles word-for-word — copying hurts your Google ranking and isn't something I can help build for legal reasons.
