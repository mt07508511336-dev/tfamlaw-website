Scottish Legal Aid Fees to Increase by 13% in September 2026

22 July 2026

We have some encouraging news for both legal professionals and those seeking legal assistance in Scotland. Starting in September 2026, solicitors providing legal aid will see a 13% increase in their fees. This adjustment is part of a broader initiative to strengthen the legal aid system and ensure that everyone has access to quality legal representation.

Legal aid is a crucial service that allows individuals who might not afford legal representation to receive the support they need. Over the years, there have been concerns about the sustainability of the legal aid system, with solicitors facing financial challenges due to stagnant fees. This increase is a positive step towards addressing these issues and ensuring that solicitors are fairly compensated for their vital work.

In addition to the fee increase, the Scottish Government is introducing funding for up to 40 Legal Aid Traineeship positions. This initiative aims to attract new talent to the field and ensure that the legal aid sector remains robust and capable of meeting the needs of the community. Furthermore, there will be digital support available to help law firms invest in technology, enhancing their efficiency and ability to serve clients effectively.

Since 2019, cumulative fee increases have amounted to just over 25%, surpassing inflation rates. The most recent rise in April 2023 brought total additional funding to £31 million since 2019. These measures reflect a commitment to maintaining a fair and accessible legal system for all.

At Thompson Family Law, we welcome these developments. We believe that a well-supported legal aid system is essential for upholding justice and ensuring that everyone, regardless of their financial situation, has access to legal support. If you have any questions about how these changes might affect you or if you need legal assistance, please don't hesitate to reach out to us.
