MPs Warn of High Risk of Miscarriages of Justice Due to Legal Aid Decline

20 July 2026

Recent findings from the House of Commons Justice Committee have raised serious concerns about the state of legal aid in England and Wales. The report highlights that the diminishing availability of legal aid and the rise in self-representation are creating an "unacceptably high risk of miscarriages of justice" and are undermining the efficient operation of the courts.

Legal aid is essential for ensuring that everyone, regardless of their financial situation, has access to legal representation. However, the committee's report indicates that many individuals are now unable to qualify for or access legal aid, and that providers are struggling to deliver services sustainably. This situation has led to an increase in individuals representing themselves in court, which can result in longer proceedings and a higher likelihood of errors.

The committee has called on the government to address several key issues, including eligibility criteria, remuneration for legal aid work, provider capacity, administrative processes, and the scope of legal aid coverage. Without urgent reform, the report warns that legal aid will continue to decline as a practical route to justice.

At Thompson Family Law, we understand the critical role that legal aid plays in upholding justice. We support the committee's recommendations and urge the government to take immediate action to restore legal aid as an effective, sustainable, and accessible service for those who need it most.

For individuals facing legal challenges, it's important to seek professional advice to understand your rights and the support available to you. Our team is committed to providing guidance and representation to ensure that justice is accessible to all.
