Scottish Legal Aid Fees to Increase by 13% This September

13 July 2026

We have some encouraging news for both legal professionals and those seeking legal assistance in Scotland. Starting this September, solicitors providing legal aid will receive a 13% increase in fees. This adjustment is part of a broader initiative to strengthen the legal aid system and ensure that quality legal support remains accessible to all.

Legal aid is a cornerstone of our justice system, offering financial assistance to individuals who might otherwise be unable to afford legal representation. This fee increase acknowledges the vital role that solicitors play in delivering these services and aims to make the provision of legal aid more sustainable for law firms.

Beyond the fee uplift, the Scottish Government has introduced additional measures to support the legal profession. These include funding for up to 40 Legal Aid Traineeship positions and financial assistance for firms to invest in digital tools. Such initiatives are designed to modernize legal practices and enhance the efficiency of legal services.

It's also worth noting that since 2019, cumulative fee increases have surpassed 25%, outpacing inflation. This demonstrates a continued commitment to supporting legal professionals and, by extension, the clients they serve.

At Thompson Family Law, we welcome these developments. They not only recognize the dedication of solicitors working within the legal aid framework but also reinforce the importance of accessible legal services for all members of our community.

For those who may be eligible for legal aid, these changes signify a strengthened support system. If you're facing legal challenges and are concerned about costs, we encourage you to reach out. Our team is here to guide you through the process and ensure you receive the assistance you need.

For more details on these updates, you can refer to the official announcement by the Scottish Government: Legal aid fees and fixed payments to rise 13%.
