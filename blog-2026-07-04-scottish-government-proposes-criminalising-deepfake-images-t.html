Scottish Government Proposes Criminalising Deepfake Images to Protect Women and Girls

4 July 2026

In a significant move to enhance protections for women and girls, the Scottish Government is considering new legislation to criminalise the creation of deepfake intimate images. This initiative aims to address the growing misuse of artificial intelligence (AI) in producing non-consensual explicit content.

Deepfake technology allows users to manipulate or generate images and videos that appear remarkably real. While this technology has legitimate applications, it has increasingly been exploited to create explicit images without the consent of the individuals depicted. Such misuse can lead to severe emotional distress and reputational damage for victims.

Currently, Scottish law addresses the sharing of intimate images without consent. However, the creation of these images using AI falls into a legal grey area. Recognising this gap, the Scottish Government has launched a consultation to explore the introduction of a specific offence targeting the creation of deepfake intimate images. The consultation also seeks views on criminalising digital tools designed solely for generating such content.

Justice Secretary Angela Constance emphasised the importance of this initiative, stating that the creation of deepfake images is "behaviour often committed by men in abusive and manipulative ways." The proposed legislation aims to deter such conduct and provide law enforcement with the necessary tools to prosecute offenders effectively.

At Thompson Family Law, we understand the profound impact that digital abuse can have on individuals and families. The proposed legislation represents a crucial step towards safeguarding personal dignity and privacy in the digital age. We encourage everyone to participate in the consultation process to ensure that the law reflects the needs and concerns of our communities.
