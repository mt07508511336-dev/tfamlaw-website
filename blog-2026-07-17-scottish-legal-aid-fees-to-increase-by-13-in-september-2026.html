Scottish Legal Aid Fees to Increase by 13% in September 2026

17 July 2026

We have some encouraging news for both legal professionals and those who rely on legal aid services in Scotland. Starting in September 2026, solicitors providing legal aid will receive a 13% increase in fees. This adjustment is part of a broader initiative aimed at bolstering the legal aid system and ensuring that quality legal assistance remains accessible to all.

In addition to the fee increase, the Scottish Government has introduced several supportive measures. These include funding for up to 40 Legal Aid Traineeship positions and financial assistance for law firms to invest in digital tools. These steps are designed to modernize legal practices and enhance the efficiency of legal services across the country.

Since 2019, cumulative fee increases have surpassed 25%, outpacing inflation and bringing total additional funding to £31 million. This sustained investment underscores a commitment to maintaining a robust and effective legal aid system in Scotland.

At Thompson Family Law, we recognize the vital role that legal aid plays in ensuring justice is accessible to everyone, regardless of their financial situation. This fee increase is a positive development that will help legal professionals continue to provide high-quality services to those in need.

For individuals seeking legal assistance, it's important to know that these enhancements aim to strengthen the support available to you. Whether you're dealing with family law matters, civil disputes, or require guidance on legal aid eligibility, our team is here to help navigate these processes with you.

As always, we remain committed to providing compassionate and practical legal support to our community. If you have any questions or need assistance, please don't hesitate to reach out to us.
